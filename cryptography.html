Multiparty Computation (MPC)

While I can't reveal the details yet, we are working on a method for improving the efficiency of Yao's garbled circuit. I constructed a proof-of-concept, writing the code in C and building off of BHR's JustGarble. Oblivious RAM

Oblivious RAM (Random Access Memory) algorithms, or ORAM algorithms, are cryptographyic algorithms designed to obscure a client's access patterns to a server. Consider when a client has their files saved on dropbox or an email repository. When a client accesses their files on the server, they access particular files in a particular sequence. It turns out that the files accessed and the sequence of their access reveals a lot of information about what the client is doing, even if the files are encrypted.

Our research was on extending exisiting ORAM algorithms to be externally verifiable. An externally verifiable ORAM algorithm allows for a third party - say a trusted friend or well paid contractor - to oversee ORAM operations and identify if a party attempts to violate protocol. This makes ORAM algorithms significantly more useful, as clients and servers can now use them with confidence that they will not be undermined or cheated.
